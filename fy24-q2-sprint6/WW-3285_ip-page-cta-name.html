Overview

Located in the centre of the historic and vibrant city of Lincoln, the University of Lincoln prides itself on its consistent reputation for outstanding student experiences and excellent teaching quality. It has recently been named as Modern University of the Year in The Times and Sunday Times Good University Guide 2021.

The quality of teaching is underpinned by a vibrant research culture where the students are able to work alongside leading academics on research projects that are making a tangible difference to society. A number of its programmes feature inspirational guest speakers and visiting professors renowned in their respective industries and professions. Some of the current students have been taught by naturalist and broadcaster Chris Packham CBE and the former Poet Laureate Dame Carol Ann Dufy.

The University of Lincoln is widely credited for its progressive collaborations with industry partners. The unique partnerships cultivated between the university and leading companies such as Siemens and Santander are an indication of this pioneering approach. This engagement is evidenced by the number of prestigious national awards that the university has secured over the last few years.

Entry requirements

The university requires at least a grade 4/C in GCSE English Language or equivalent for all courses, and some have further specific requirements. Entry requirements for individual courses can be found on the University of Lincoln website.

The University of Lincoln accepts a wide range of qualifications as the basis for entry including A levels, T levels, BTEC Diplomas, and a variety of other Diplomas including European and International Baccalaureate Diplomas. The university also makes efforts to consider applicants with alternative qualifications and those with professional or other relevant work experience.

Students are encouraged to contact the universitys admissions office to discuss their unique admissions situation if they require advice or guidance. Every application made to the university is considered on an individual basis. Offers made by the university to applicants are at the discretion of the University of Lincoln. The Admissions Team welcomes queries and questions regarding applicants qualifications prior to applications being formally made.

Accommodation

Choosing where to live is a big decision. At Lincoln, the university guarantees a bedroom in a university residence to all new students who apply for their accommodation by 1 September in the year of their entry (e.g. by 1 September 2021 for students looking to join in autumn 2021).

The university offers a range of options to meet different needs and budgets, and its Accommodation Services Team is on hand to help students explore their options. Based in the heart of Lincolns city centre, most of the universitys student accommodation is situated on its Brayford Pool Campus or just a short distance away. Thanks to its fantastic location, no matter which accommodation students choose, they will never be far from the action.

All university accommodation is supported by its Residential Warden Team who are there to support and advise students, helping them to settle in and make the most of their time at Lincoln. The universitys online application system for accommodation is currently open for students who have formally accepted their academic offer to study in autumn 2021.

Facilities

The University of Lincoln places a high value on providing its students with the best possible foundation from which they can work towards their ambitions. Regardless of the study programme chosen, the university strives to ensure that students have great access to the best and most cutting-edge equipment and resources to support their learning.

The University of Lincoln has recently unveiled its new Isaac Newton building, made possible through 28m of investments, and its 19m Sarah Swift facility. A new and specialist medical school is soon to be completed and will facilitate the outstanding education and training of many new doctors and medical staff.

Across the university, facilities have been designed with students in mind and with close attention to their subject-specific requirements. Whether its modern and engaging learning spaces, social spaces or collaborative working environments, students will find that their campus environment actively enhances their ability to learn and grow. In addition to this, a huge range of specialist facilities are available to allow students to reach the top of their subject areas - ranging from robotics labs to clinical suites, from animal behaviour labs to an Electro-Magnetic Chamber.

The universitys campus was recently accredited with a Gold award from EcoCampus due to its ongoing work in pioneering sustainability and good environmental management across the campus.

Student support

3.8 Student support rating

While completing their studies, students at the university will have access to support from their personal tutor across the academic year. This tutor will be able to provide guidance, advice, support and motivation to help students make the most of their university experience. The University of Lincoln also operates a range of support services for students that relate to academic or personal matters, to help students make the most of their time at university.

Many of these services are centralised in the Student Support Centre, located at the heart of the Brayford Pool Campus. Help is available for a range of topics, from fees, funding, and making payments, to housing, visas, and legal advice.

The health and wellbeing of the universitys students is of the utmost importance. As soon as students have enrolled, they are able to register with the universitys on-campus Health Centre, which specialises in students health and offers appointments with NHS doctors and nurses. Alternatively, the university can provide the details of other GP practices based within the city.

Students can request the specialist advice of professional staff in the universitys Student Wellbeing Centre. The team includes student wellbeing advisors, mental health advisors, and counsellors who are able to provide advice and guidance in relation to disability, medical conditions, learning differences, and mental health.

Academic strengths

The University of Lincoln takes great pride in the outstanding quality of its teaching, which is evidenced by its Gold Award via the national Teaching Excellence Framework. In addition to this, the expertise of staff at the university is frequently augmented by a range of prestigious and well- respected visiting staff. They come from a variety of backgrounds and professions and provide valuable insight and lessons on the professional world. They include the former Poet Laureate Dame Carol Ann Duffy, and Chris Packham CBE.

At the University of Lincoln, excellent teaching is underpinned by a pioneering and ambitious research culture. The university is proud of its Student as Producer initiative which greatly enhances the teaching, learning and engagement capacities of students. It gives students the opportunity to act as collaborators in the generation of new knowledge.

In addition, students have the chance to work on pioneering research projects, alongside expert academics who are changing lives through groundbreaking studies. Many courses offer opportunities for students to develop their research skills and gain hands-on experience in a variety of contexts and settings.

Students' Union

Working together with the University of Lincolns Students Union (an independent organisation run by elected officers on behalf of students at Lincoln), the university offers an array of events, activities, and amenities designed to help students settle in to university life, meet friends, and make the most of their time at Lincoln.

Students can enjoy a range of virtual and on-campus events and activities throughout the year, including live music, comedy, quizzes, and fairs.

The Students Union (SU) operates several major on-campus venues which are popular hotspots for students during the day and at night. It includes one of the regions largest live venues and has welcomed music acts such as Kaiser Chiefs, Kings of Leon, Kasabian, and Rudimental, as well as comedians including Sarah Millican, Romesh Ranganathan, and Katherine Ryan.

For students who may need help or support during their studies, the SU runs its own Advice Centre offering free, independent, and impartial advice on a range of topics, including academic, financial, housing, and welfare issues. The SU also engages in campaigns throughout the year, talking about important issues and raising awareness of BAME, LGBT+, womens, disabled, and environmental issues.

Clubs and Societies

Taking part in a sporting activities, joining a club, or becoming the newest member of a student society is an excellent way to make fast friends and form bonds which can last far beyond graduation day. The Students Union at the University of Lincoln provides access to more than 150 different societies and sporting activities and students are free to join any society of their choosing.

Societies at the University of Lincoln are incredibly diverse, and include Yoga, Anime, Harry Potter, Basketball, 5-a-side football, and more.

On the campus itself, the universitys Sports Centre supports a number of facilities that are used by Students Union sports clubs and include football pitches, basketball and squash courts, trampolines and hockey pitches. There is also a range of classes and gym passes to access.

City life

One of the worlds great historic cities, Lincoln is renowned for its fusion of old and new. The winning combination of a vibrant city centre and a historic Cathedral Quarter, both offering a rich variety of attractions, activities, and amenities, makes Lincoln a fantastic place in which to live and study.

Just a short walk from campus, students will find a range of restaurants, cafes, and bars, and the citys high street and side streets offer a mix of independent and boutique stores alongside national brands and chains.

Step back in time along the citys famous cobbled Steep Hill, which provides the gateway to the citys historic Cathedral Quarter and Bailgate area, where character-filled streets are lined with quirky shops and enticing eateries. The area is home to Lincoln Cathedral, one of Europes most celebrated medieval buildings and Lincoln Castle, which holds one of only four surviving originals of the 1215 Magna Carta.

Students living in Lincoln will find that they dont need to travel very far to find the great outdoors. There are a number of parks and picturesque waterways within the city itself, while beautiful countryside and coastal landscapes are just a stones throw away.  It is a great city for getting outside and active.

Job prospects

3.5 Career prospects rating

The University of Lincoln has a team of employability and careers professionals ready to help students develop their knowledge, skills, confidence, and experience needed to kick-start their chosen career.

The universitys advisors offer individual support and advice through a combination of face-to-face and online one-to-one appointments, group sessions, and workshops. Daily drop-in sessions with the Careers Clinic are also available. The team is on hand to answer queries and help students develop practical skills, such as building their CV and preparing for interviews.

A range of employment advice and skills development workshops are delivered throughout the year as part of a calendar of events which allow students to engage with top employers.

Students can sign up to the universitys CareerLinc service to receive updates on student employment, work placement, and graduate-level opportunities with a range of regional, national, and global organisations. The Campus Jobs service can help students to find part-time work to fit around their studies.

The university is proud to have developed innovative partnerships with a range of organisations. It aims to provide students with enhanced opportunities for work experience, networking, and access to latest industry trends. Many of the courses offer the chance to gain hands-on experience through work placements and professional practice years. Opportunities may be available both in the UK and overseas via the universitys Global Opportunities Team.

Scholarships/ Bursaries

The University of Lincoln believes that higher education has an extremely transformative impact on peoples lives. Each year the university accepts students from a wide range of backgrounds from across the world and provides access to a variety of scholarships and bursaries for eligible students.

Almost half of new UK undergraduates at the University of Lincoln currently qualify for the University of Lincoln Scholarship, which is worth 1,500 and is spread over three years of study. The university also offers a 1,000 Excellence Scholarship which recognises and rewards outstanding academic achievement amongst new undergraduates across its four academic colleges.

The university offers a wide range of scholarships to provide financial assistance to suitably qualified international students who come to study at Lincoln. There are also support options for postgraduate level study.

Fees

Going to university is a pivotal step in anyones life, and it is essential that students understand the costs involved and the variety of funding pathways available to students before they start. As there are no fees to pay upfront for eligible undergraduates from the UK, a university education is achievable for everyone. The Advice Centre at the University of Lincoln is on hand to help students, offering them a wide range of information and support regarding all aspects of financing their studies.

At the University of Lincoln, tuition fees for UK students for the 2021 academic year are 9,250 per year for full-time undergraduate entry, or 77 per credit point if undertaking part-time study. The majority of students at the university choose to cover the cost of their full-time fees through applying for a Tuition Fee Loan from the Student Loans Company.

Frequently asked questions

Why go to University of Lincoln?

As well as a wide range of courses, studying at University of Lincoln offers you loads of opportunities to boost your CV thanks to the careers department. You might even have the chance to do a work placement or sandwich year during your degree.

As well as providing you with opportunities to develop academically and professionally, you'll have a wide range of clubs and societies to join. You'll be able to pick up a new hobby and make loads of new friends by joining some of the clubs and societies on offer at University of Lincoln.

What can you do with a degree from University of Lincoln?

The world is your oyster! Of course, the exact options open to you will depend on which subject you choose to study. Employers often look to hire graduates, so you can be sure that your degree will give you a wider range of employment opportunities.

No matter what degree you choose, however, you'll have option of going on to further study. If you want to keep studying, your undergraduate degree will enable you to go on to a master's degree or even a PhD.

What accommodation does University of Lincoln offer?

If you opt to live in uni halls, you'll benefit from free WiFi and all-inclusive rent. Rent for uni halls also includes security and maintenance, so you can be sure you'll be well-looked after. There are several accessible rooms available, which are adapted for disabled students.

Halls also have communal areas such as kitchen facilities where you can meet your flatmates over a cup of tea. You'll also have access to dining facilities where you can share a meal with other students in your accommodation.
